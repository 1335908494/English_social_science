Q1: What do both of the videos tell you about reading a paper? 
A1: The different sections that an academic article contains and the role each section plays.
Q2: What section do you have to read of an academic article? 
A2: The title, the abstract, the keywords, the introduction and the conclusion.
Q3: What section don’t you have to read? 
A3: The findings, the methodology and the discussion.
Q4: In what order should you read a research paper?
A4: At first, we had better read it in the order of the title, the keywords, the abstract, the conclusion and the introduction. After we make sure that it’s useful, we should read it carefully in the chronological order.
